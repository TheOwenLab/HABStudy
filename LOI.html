Invitation to Participate

You are being invited to participate in a research study because you are an adult 18 years or older. The purpose of this study is to investigate what amount of age-related cognitive change is considered normal, and to relate levels of cognitive functioning, measured with a collection of online tests, to other lifestyle variables such as nutrition, physical activity, sleep, and mental health.

Why is this study being done?

The early stages and milder side of cognitive decline have been dismissed as inevitable parts of growing older, but that narrative is changing. It is also unclear whether there are certain protective lifestyle factors or flags that clinicians can use to predict who is on track for healthy brain aging, and who is not. The Healthy Aging Brain Study will follow the same participants over a year to more precisely measure how cognition changes with age, helping us understand what healthy cognitive aging entails, and what lifestyle factors are associated with cognitive resilience.

What are the study procedures?

You will be eligible to participate in this study if you:

Inclusion Criteria:
  • Have access to a laptop, desktop, or tablet computing device with an active internet connection.

You will not be eligible to participate in this study if you:

Exclusion Criteria:
  • Are less than 18 years of age.
  • Are unable to read English.
  • Have any motor difficulties.

After reading through this letter, and if you are eligible and agree to participate, you will be asked to sign up using your email address. Within 5 to 15 minutes, an email will be sent to your address that contains a link for you to begin the study.

You will be brought to a website portal where you will be asked to complete a short demographic questionnaire that includes general questions about yourself (e.g., your age, level of education, etc.) which will take approximately 3 minutes.

After this, you will be presented with options to complete any or all of the study procedures. These include cognitive tests and additional questionnaires, organized into the following modules:

Module 1 - Healthy Aging ~10 minutes
  • 2 cognitive tasks (Feature Match, Number Ladder)
  • 2 questionnaires (IQCODE and IADL)
Module 2 - Everyday Memory ~10 minutes
  • 2 cognitive tasks (Digit Span, Paired Associates)
  • 1 health questionnaire (HABS Health Questionnaire)
Module 3 - Planning & Strategy ~10 minutes
  • 2 cognitive tasks (Spatial Planning, Token Search)
  • 2 mental health questionnaires (PHQ-9, GAD-7)
Module 4 - Attention ~6 minutes
  • Sustained attention to respond task (SART)
Module 5 - Verbal ~10 minutes
  • 2 cognitive tasks (Grammatical Reasoning, Double Trouble)
  • 1 extended demographics questionnaire (HABS Full Demographic Questionnaire)
Module 6 - Reasoning Puzzles ~7 minutes
  • 2 cognitive tasks (Odd One Out, Rotations)
Module 7 - Spatial Abilities ~5 minutes
  • 2 cognitive tasks (Polygons, Spatial Span)
Module 8 - Lifestyle ~10 minutes
  • 1 lifestyle questionnaire (Healthy Aging Activity Engagement Scale)
Additional Information ~10 minutes
  • 1 Open-Ended Response (“Tell us about your healthy habits.”)

Each cognitive task takes anywhere from 90 seconds to a few minutes each, and is designed to be engaging and easy to play, while providing measures related to a specific cognitive function, like working memory, attention, or executive functioning.

Once you have completed a module, you will be shown a summary of your performance. If you complete any of Modules #1 - 7, you will be given information about your cognitive scores (e.g., on which game did you get the best score?). If you complete Module #8, you will be given your Healthy Aging Activity Engagement Scale lifestyle report score, which classifies lifestyle into categories. When a module is completed, visual elements on the website portal will update to indicate which modules are complete and which modules are not complete. Specifically an image of the human brain will become “filled in” as you complete modules. Note that this report is not, and should not be mistaken for, a cognitive assessment or diagnostic tool. If you are concerned about your cognitive health, you should talk to your healthcare provider.

Completed modules can be revisited at any time to view results for that module. If all (optional) aspects of the study are completed, the study will take a total time of about 75 minutes to complete.

You can take breaks through the study, if you wish, but the Creyos cognitive tasks should be completed within 2 hours of starting them. If you do not finish the study, we may send you a reminder email to pick up where you left off. Please note that the questionnaires and cognitive tests need to be completed on either a laptop, computer, or tablet.

About one year after you first signed up, we will email you about completing the same modules again, to see how your lifestyle and brain health have changed.

Are there any risks to participating in this study?

There are no known risks associated with this research study; however, as with any online related activity, the risk of a privacy breach is always possible. If any questions make you feel uncomfortable, you can choose not to answer them.

Additionally, since you will be given information about your performance after completing the study modules, it is important to recognize that these scores do not constitute formal medical results or advice. If you have concerns about your cognitive or general health status, we urge you to contact your doctor.

Benefits

There are no known benefits to you associated with participating in this research study. The data collected from this study will investigate the relationships between certain lifestyle factors and cognition, and whether these associations are consistent across the lifespan. Importantly, this information might inform policy guidelines to encourage activities that promote healthy cognition.

Compensation

There is no compensation for participating in this study. The more study modules you complete, the more information you will receive about your cognitive performance, brain health, and lifestyle.

Voluntary participation

We will update you with new information during the study that might affect your decision to continue with your participation in the study.

Participation in this study is voluntary. You may refuse to participate, refuse to answer any questions and/or withdraw from the study at any time by closing your browser window. Some questionnaire items—specifically those about your age, sex, gender, and highest level of education—cannot be skipped; these items are important because they have been shown to be related to performance on cognitive tasks. You may choose to complete as many modules as you’d like. Once started, the Creyos tasks cannot be skipped, however, you can choose to withdraw by simply closing your browser window.

If you choose to withdraw from or do not complete the study, your data will remain secured in the Creyos and Qualtrics databases unless you specifically request for us to remove your data; then, all study data associated with your participant ID in our systems will be deleted. Contact the research team at owenlab@uwo.ca to request that your data be deleted. You do not waive any legal right by consenting to this study.

Withdrawal from study

If you change your mind about participating in the study, you can stop answering the questions at any time by closing the browser. The data already collected as part of the study will nonetheless be preserved, analyzed, or used to ensure the completeness of the study. If you wish to have your data removed before any study publication, you can contact owenlab@uwo.ca and provide your participant ID or email address which will be used to identify and remove your data from Qualtrics, Creyos, and Western data servers. It will not be possible to remove your data from any published manuscripts or datasets, but we will still remove your data from any sources where it can be identified from your participant ID (i.e., Creyos and Qualtrics data servers).

Confidentiality

After agreeing to participate in this study we will ask for your email address. This is required so that: 1) we can ensure that people only participate once; 2) we can re-contact you for 1-year follow-up assessment; 3) we can send recovery links in case you encounter technical difficulties; and 4) we can provide updates directly related to this study. Your email address is the only identifying piece of information that we will ask you about, and it will not be shared with any other entities. Your name, street address, phone number, etc. are not required, will not be asked for, and will not be stored on any server, or by any service, or used to collect data for this study. Also, none of the platforms used for this study record your location or IP address.

You will automatically be assigned a unique alphanumeric participant identifier, something like “bzk8daobgu4g3dvujzq0px3ryka”, when you agree to participate in this study. This ID will be stored alongside your email address in a master list available only to the co-investigators of this study. Your unique ID will be used to track your progress and to link the data you provide across different questionnaires. This is all done automatically, but you will be asked to copy down your identifier in case you need it to recover your progress. Your questionnaire data and cognitive test data (collected via Creyos) will only be associated with this participant ID.

Questionnaire (survey) data, including your email address, will be collected through a secure online survey platform called Qualtrics. Qualtrics uses encryption technology and restricted access authorizations to protect data (https://www.qualtrics.com/security-statement/). In addition, Western’s Qualtrics server is in Ireland, where privacy standards are maintained under the European Union safe harbour framework. By participating in this study, you are agreeing to Qualtrics’ privacy policy (https://www.qualtrics.com/privacy-statement/).

Your cognitive test data, linked only to your unique participant identifier, will be kept on a secure database server hosted by Creyos. Creyos uses encryption technology and restricted access authorizations to protect all data. Your data will remain confidential and will be accessible only to the investigators of this study and internal members of Creyos (for quality assurance purposes). In order to start the cognitive tests that are part of this study, you must agree to Creyos’s terms of use (https://creyos.com/terms-of-service) and privacy policy (https://creyos.com/privacy-policy). Note that the research team, considered a third party according to Creyos’s privacy policy, possesses only your email address.

When the study is complete, cognitive testing data and questionnaire data will be exported to a secure server at Western University for analysis. All analyses will be performed on data that does not contain directly identifying information; that is, your email address will not be a part of any exported datasets. Only your unique participant ID will be used to link your data. Any publications from this study will present aggregate statistics and any final datasets associated with these publications will be anonymized by removing all participant identifiers.

Unless you request that it be removed earlier, all study data will be kept for 7 years on Western University’s secure server and will then be destroyed. Prior to that, your data can be permanently deleted from Creyos’s, Qualtrics’, and Western’s servers upon request by providing your participant ID to the research team. Note that your data cannot be removed from any manuscripts and final datasets (that have been fully anonymized), once they have been published.

Open access data

It is becoming common for researchers to make their study data publicly available to other researchers in order to facilitate open and replicable science. The research team supports this initiative and will make a final dataset available to other researchers. Your individual data will appear in this dataset, but it will be fully anonymized by removing participant IDs. That is, none of your identifying information will be part of the final dataset.

This database will be stored on Borealis—Western University's Dataverse (https://borealisdata.ca/dataverse/westernu)—a research data repository for faculty, students, and staff. The data repository will contain the final dataset comprising fully anonymized study data - including cognitive test scores, plus demographic and other questionnaire data. It will be publicly available to any researchers who sign the guestbook indicating their affiliations and intended use.
